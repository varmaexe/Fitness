## 4. Form Notes

**Bulgarian Split Squat — "More challenging in lower back than quads"**
This is a torso angle problem. When the torso leans too far forward in a BSS, the load transfers from the front quad to the hip extensors and lumbar. The quad is a knee extensor — it only gets challenged when the shin is vertical and the torso is **upright**. Fixes:
- Move the front foot further forward (further than you think — toes should be ~2–3 feet from the bench)
- Drive your front knee over your toes actively
- Keep your chest up, eyes forward — do not hinge at the hip
- If 17.5kg feels too heavy to maintain form upright, drop to 12.5kg and do it right. No point loading a lower back movement.

**Seated Leg Curl → Lying Leg Curl switch — "Don't feel the position on machine right"**
Valid call to switch. The seated leg curl puts the hamstring in a shortened position (hip flexed), which can feel uncomfortable and creates less tension through full ROM for some people. Lying leg curls are biomechanically more forgiving and are the standard starting point. The problem is you only logged 1 working set on lying curls — that's not enough volume. Next session, lying leg curls get 3 full working sets.
